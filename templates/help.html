User Manual

Manifest is the e-signature platform for the DockLabs suite. It captures multi-party signatures on PDFs through configurable, ordered workflows with full audit trails.

Manifest is also the cross-product signing handoff layer: peer products (Harbor, Bounty, Helm, etc.) send documents here for signature, and Manifest returns the signed PDF and a completion event when done.

A Flow is a reusable template — an ordered list of signers, the documents to sign, and the signature placements on those documents. The same "Grant Award Agreement" flow can drive hundreds of signing sessions.

A Packet is an active instance of a flow being executed for a specific entity. Packets carry status (Draft / In Progress / Completed / Cancelled / Declined), signer progress, and the final signed PDF.

Steps execute sequentially. When a packet is initiated, step 1 becomes ACTIVE and the assigned signer receives an email link. Steps 2+ stay PENDING until each prior step is signed.

When the last required step is signed, Manifest generates the final signed PDF, marks the packet Completed, and (if the packet came from a peer product) posts the signed PDF back to that product's webhook.

Three places:

  1. The email you received contains a link to /sign/<step_id>/. Click it.
  2. From inside Manifest, visit My Signatures in the sidebar — every step where you're the active signer is listed there.
  3. Helm's dashboard Awaiting Me → Manifest column lists every step you owe across the suite.

Each route lands you on the same signing page: the PDF with your fields highlighted, a signature capture pad (type / draw / upload), and the Sign or Decline buttons.

Click Decline on the signing page and provide a reason (required). Your step transitions to DECLINED and the packet is marked Declined — terminal. Other signers stop receiving prompts.

If the packet came from a peer product, the originating workflow is notified so staff can address the issue and (if appropriate) initiate a new packet.

Visit Saved Signatures from the sidebar user menu (or /my/signatures/). Click New signature, pick a type (typed / drawn / uploaded), give it a label like "Formal" or "Initials", and save.

Mark one signature as default and it pre-populates the signing pad next time you sign. You can have multiple saved signatures — useful if you sign in different contexts.

Open a Document inside a Flow and click Edit placements (or visit /documents/<id>/placements/). Manifest renders the PDF and lets you drag fields onto each page:

  • Signature — captured signature image.
  • Initials — short capture.
  • Date — auto-stamped at signing.
  • Printed Name — typed name.

Each placement is bound to a specific step (signer). Coordinates are stored as percentages so positions stay correct at any zoom level.

Peer products (Harbor, Bounty, Helm, etc.) call keel.signatures.client.send_to_manifest() with a source object, flow, and signer list. Manifest creates a packet, runs it through its normal signing sequence, and when the packet completes posts the signed PDF back to the source product's webhook.

The source product's packet_approved handler attaches the signed PDF, advances its workflow status, and registers the file for FOIA. The link from peer to packet is captured as the packet's source entity and rendered in the Packet detail header.

Yes. Manifest is a fully functional e-signature platform on its own — flows, documents, placements, packets, signing, and audit all work without any peer products deployed.

In standalone mode, signer roles are managed at /roles/ instead of being sourced from suite SSO claims, and packets are initiated and consumed entirely inside Manifest.

Open the packet and click Audit (or visit /packets/<id>/audit/). Staff see every event — packet initiation, step activation, signature capture (with IP and timestamp), declines, reminders, and PDF generation — as immutable rows.

The trail is what gives ESIGN / UETA-compliant evidence of consent and process. Audit rows are written to Keel's immutable AuditLog and are FOIA-exportable.

Yes. Staff can visit /packets/<id>/cancel/ and provide a reason. The packet is marked Cancelled (terminal) and signers can no longer sign their steps.

Cancellation is logged in the audit trail with the actor and reason. To start over, initiate a new packet from the same flow.

Open your sidebar profile dropdown and click Notification Preferences (or visit /notifications/preferences/).

For each event type — signature_required, signature_reminder, signing_complete, signing_declined — toggle the in-app and email channels independently. Critical alerts are delivered regardless of preferences.
